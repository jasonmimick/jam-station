Contributor Guide

Sending your music to the station

You found music worth sharing. Here's how it gets onto the family radio — straight from your Mac to the always-on station, over a private link that stays only ever between our own machines.

Set up once Ten minutes, one time. After this, sending music is a single command.
1

Install Tailscale and sign in

I'll email you a Tailscale invite. Get the app from tailscale.com/download, install it, then open the invite and sign in with your Google account — the same email the invite came to. That's the whole setup. This quietly puts your Mac on our own private network — no ports to open, no router settings, nothing exposed to the internet. Just our machines, able to reach each other directly.

2

Confirm you can see the station

Open Terminal and run this. You should see jasons-mac-mini in the list — that's the computer the radio lives on.

$ tailscale status
Every time Whenever you've got something new for the dial.
3

Name a folder the way the station should read

Put the tracks in one folder, and name that folder exactly what you want the station to be called — the folder name becomes the station name.

Guitar Festival Chicago 2007 becomes a station called Guitar Festival Chicago 2007 on the dial.

4

Send the folder

One command copies the whole folder over the private link into the station's inbox. Big folders are fine — it's a direct connection, so there's no upload limit, and if it's interrupted you just run it again and it picks up where it left off.

$ rsync -av "Guitar Festival Chicago 2007" jason@jasons-mac-mini:jam-inbox/

What happens next

Within a minute, it's a station.

The station notices the new folder, reads the tracks, and puts it on the dial as its own station — name and all. Anyone tuned into the family radio can turn to it and listen. You just added to the airwaves from your kitchen table.

Coming next

Run your own station

Right now you're a contributor — you send music, it lands on the family radio. The next step is becoming a real broadcaster: your own sovereign station, on your own machine, with your own dial and your own listeners — and the family network simply agrees to carry it. Your station, your music, your call. That part's on the way.

A few honest notes

  • Non-commercial music only — your own recordings, live tapings, out-of-print and creative-commons material. Not ripped commercial albums for public play.
  • Your files stay yours. The link is private to our machines over Tailscale; nothing is public unless it goes on the dial, and even then it's the broadcast that travels, not your files.
  • Send as much as you like — a single show or a whole shelf. Each top-level folder becomes its own station.
